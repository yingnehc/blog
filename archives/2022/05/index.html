Place-Based Policies: First-Mover Advantage and Persistence, with Teng Huang and Wenzhuo Lu, under review

Abstract: Researchers have studied various place-based policies aimed at promoting economic growth, but little attention has been paid to how timing and policy contexts can foster agglomeration. In this study, we use a general equilibrium framework to examine how productivity spillovers resulting from a policy-driven foreign entry in early development zones can affect firms’ decisions on technology upgrading, production locations, and firm sizes. Guided by the model’s predictions, we collected comprehensive archival data and empirically demonstrated heterogeneous growth trends between early and late development zones. These trends are exhibited in persistent firm entries, innovation, and labor pooling.
