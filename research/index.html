Working papers

Food Delivery Platforms and Urban Consumption Amenities: Evidence from China, with Shimeng Liu

Abstract: This paper provides the first empirical evidence that food delivery platforms change the landscape of urban consumption amenities. Applying a difference-in-differences framework to a novel granular dataset with proprietary information, we study the impacts of food delivery platforms on local cuisine diversity and the economic geography of restaurants in China. We find that food delivery platforms increase the number of restaurants, restaurant entries and exits, and cuisine diversity while reducing restaurants’ spatial concentration and price levels. The effects are nonlinear across counties of different market sizes and degrees of urbanization, narrowing the core-peripheral gap in accessibility to consumption amenities.

Place-Based Policies: First-Mover Advantage and Persistence, with Teng Huang, Wenzhuo Lu, and Xiaochen Xie

Abstract: Researchers have studied various place-based policies aimed at promoting economic growth, but little attention has been paid to how timing and policy contexts can foster agglomeration. In this study, we develop a framework to understand the impact of the accumulation of productivity resulting from a policy-driven foreign entry in early development zones on labor migration and firm investment in subsequent periods. Guided by the model’s predictions, we collected comprehensive archival data and empirically demonstrated heterogeneous growth trends between early and late development zones. These trends are exhibited in persistent firm entries, innovation, and labor pooling.
